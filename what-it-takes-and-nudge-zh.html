读后感：《苏世民：我的经验与教训》《助推》

by Iris - Tue, 12 Sep 2023
Tags: #Snippets

《苏世民：我的经验与教训》

It’s at these inflection points that the most important lessons in business and life are learned.

What It Takes《苏世民：我的经验与教训》在我的人生转折点上给了我很多启示，比如

Regardless of how you begin your careers, it is important to realize that your life will not necessarily move in a straight line.

抛开代笔的影响，这本书的口吻和观念带着美国式的激进，但不失谦逊。苏世民本人非常看重教育，他相信“education is the passport to a better life”（教育是通向更好生活的通行证），并且认为教育的意义在于让人有质疑和思考的能力：

I believe that education is a discipline. The object of this discipline is to learn how to think. Once we have mastered this, we can use it to learn a vocation, appreciate art, or read a book. Education simply enables us to appreciate the ever-changing drama fashioned of God’s own hand, life itself. Education continues when we leave the classroom. Our associations with friends, our participation in clubs all increase our store of knowledge. In fact, we never stop learning until we die. My fellow officers and I just hope that you will become aware of the purpose of education and follow its basic tenets, questioning and thinking, for the rest of your life.

从宾州的布料商店，到短跑选手，到推翻耶鲁校规，到主动拜访大佬Averell Harriman，到出海，到从军，到质疑哈佛MBA太水，到卖掉雷曼，到创立黑石。他有野心，有眼力，也有底线。底线就是不管市场好坏，永远用对的方法做对的事，不要让投资人亏钱。就算亏钱了，也要自掏腰包，负责：

But there are times when you just have to stand up and write a check. You have to show customers that you own this, because if you don’t, they’re not going to trust you ever again.

他真的很真诚。愿意倾听，解决别人的问题（其实就是解决自己的问题）

In any conversation with someone you don’t know, you should always be patient and keep asking questions until you find a place of common ground.

There is nothing more interesting to people than their own problems. If you can find out what they are and come up with solutions, they will want to talk to you no matter their rank or status.

因为本科学的是行为学，他常常会探究人的心理，这点人情味让他在金融业和政界如鱼得水。知心而明鉴，明鉴而智行。

Hearing the nuance in someone’s voice or seeing that person’s body language told me as much as anything he or she said.

While most investors say they are interested in making money, they are actually interested in psychological comfort. They would rather be part of the herd, even when the herd is losing money, than make the hard decisions that yield the greatest rewards.

Finance is an industry that encourages self-delusion.

真的好想去苏世民书院进修啊～

《助推》

Thaler has long said that he hopes a day will come when the field of behavioral economics no longer exists because economists will incorporate the existence of Humans in their analyses to whatever extent that is appropriate (or even optimal) . (pp.336)

Nudge《助推》是一本有关行为经济学的书，用风趣、实用的方式谈学术。

在传统经济学中，总是假设人是理性的，行为受动机驱使：

Econs are pretty unsociable creatures. They communicate with others only if they can gain something from the encounter, they care about their reputations (because a good reputation is valuable), and they will learn from others if actual information can be obtained, but Econs are not followers of fashion. Their hemlines do not go up and down except for practical reasons, they do not wear ties, and if they did they would not grow narrower and wider simply as a matter of style. (pp.64) （我很像以上段落中的Econ）

但是现实中，人（Humans）会有很多心理或者行为上的偏差/失误，让许多经济学理论失效。因为人是人，所以他们需要外力去助推他们实现理性行为，比如投资养老金、节能减排、不乱刷信用卡、坚持锻炼等等。作者把这种助推的理念称之为自由主义的父权主义（Libertarian Paternalism），只有Econs才能想出来的词。

The golden rule of libertarian paternalism: offer nudges that are most likely to help and least likely to inflict harm. (pp.91)

People are most likely to need nudges when decisions require scarce attention, when decisions are difficult, when people do not get prompt feedback, and when they have trouble translating aspects of the situation into terms that they can easily understand. (pp.91)

助推的关键在于设计选择结构（Choice Architecture），最好的方法就是让对的选择变简单（Make It Easy）！比如，自助餐把沙拉放在最前面，引导人们健康饮食；让人在申请驾照的时候决定是否捐献器官；把投资养老金设置为默认选项等等。

书中的观点给政策设计提供了很多启示，简而言之就是让政策人性化，既考虑到人性的弱点，也在执行时助推人的行为，让一切变简单，而不是制造很多sludge来阻碍执行。

同时，书里的行为偏差（mental accounting, loss aversion, etc）也对个人的消费行为进行了剖析。基于这些研究，我建成了自己的理财模型-貔貅，助推自己早日买上金条。
